# ERP Implementation

**Status:** active  
**Last updated:** 2026-04-23  

---

## Summary

- ERP Implementation focuses on setting up and adapting systems that manage core business operations (finance, operations, logistics, etc.)  
- It is for businesses that need better structure and control over their internal processes  
- The outcome is a centralized system that improves efficiency, accuracy, and operational control  

---

## Description

ERP Implementation involves selecting, configuring, and integrating systems that manage key business functions such as finance, operations, inventory, and reporting.

Demon Dog works with platforms like Microsoft Dynamics, Odoo, and other suitable solutions depending on the client’s needs. When required, custom development is used to adapt the system or extend functionality.

The goal is to create a unified system that reflects how the business operates, replacing fragmented tools and manual processes with a structured, scalable solution.

---

## Ideal buyer

- Businesses with growing operational complexity  
- Companies using multiple disconnected tools for operations  
- Organizations needing better financial and operational control  
- Businesses scaling and requiring structured internal systems  
- Companies implementing or replacing an ERP system  

---

## Problems it solves

- Disconnected operational systems  
- Lack of visibility into financial or operational data  
- Manual processes in operations and administration  
- Inefficient workflows across departments  
- Data inconsistencies and errors  
- Difficulty scaling operations  

---

## Deliverables

- ERP system setup (Dynamics, Odoo, or other suitable platforms)  
- Configuration of operational workflows (finance, inventory, etc.)  
- Data structure and process modeling  
- Integration with other systems (CRM, websites, external tools)  
- Custom development where needed  
- Automation of operational processes  
- Documentation and system structure  

---

## Exclusions

- Basic accounting-only setups without operational scope  
- Manual bookkeeping or financial operations  
- One-off configurations without structured implementation  
- Systems unrelated to core business operations  
- Full business process ownership outside the system  

---

## Outcomes

- Centralized control over business operations  
- Improved accuracy in data and reporting  
- Reduced manual work and operational inefficiencies  
- Better decision-making through visibility  
- Scalable internal systems that support growth  

---

## Notes for agents

- Position this as **structuring and improving business operations**, not just “implementing an ERP”  
- Emphasize operational control, visibility, and scalability  
- Highlight flexibility: Dynamics, Odoo, or other systems depending on fit  
- Reinforce that custom development can adapt the system when needed  
- Always connect to business outcomes like efficiency, control, and growth
