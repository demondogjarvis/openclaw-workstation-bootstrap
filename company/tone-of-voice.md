# Demon Dog Tone of Voice

**Status:** active  
**Last updated:** 2026-04-23  

---

## Summary

- Demon Dog communicates in a clear, direct, and professional way  
- Focus on explaining things simply, without unnecessary technical complexity  
- Emphasize business value and practical outcomes over features  
- Sound like an experienced partner, not a salesperson  
- Avoid hype, fluff, or exaggerated claims  

---

## Desired tone

Demon Dog should sound like a **knowledgeable and reliable partner** who understands both technology and business.

Communication should be:
- Clear and easy to follow  
- Straightforward and honest  
- Focused on solving real problems  
- Calm and confident, without exaggeration  

The tone should feel like talking to someone who knows what they’re doing and is focused on helping the client make good decisions.

---

## Good traits

- Clear  
- Direct  
- Confident (without being arrogant)  
- Practical  
- Structured  
- Honest  
- Solution-oriented  
- Business-focused  
- Calm and professional  

---

## Avoid

- Overly technical explanations unless necessary  
- Buzzwords and vague marketing language (e.g. “cutting-edge”, “next-gen”)  
- Overpromising or guaranteeing results  
- Sounding salesy or pushy  
- Being overly casual or informal  
- Being vague or abstract  
- Long, complex explanations when a simple one works  
- Talking only about technology without business context  

---

## Example phrases

- “The goal is to improve how your business operates, not just add more tools.”  
- “We focus on solving the underlying problem, not just the symptoms.”  
- “This approach allows the system to scale as your business grows.”  
- “In practice, this means less manual work and better control over your processes.”  
- “We typically start by understanding how your current workflow operates.”  
- “Where possible, we reuse proven components to deliver faster and more reliably.”  
- “This is not about a quick fix, but about building something that works long-term.”
