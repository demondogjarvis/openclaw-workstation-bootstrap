# Case Study: LRF Kassan

**Client:** LRF (Lantbrukarnas Riksförbund)  
**Project:** LRF Kassan  
**Status:** draft  
**Last updated:** 2026-04-23  

---

## Summary

- Built a centralized platform to manage budgets, expenses, and compensation for LRF’s local groups and representatives  
- Designed to support a large, distributed organization with many stakeholders and varying financial responsibilities  
- Outcome: improved financial control, reduced manual work, and enabled local groups to actively manage and grow their budgets  

---

## About the client

LRF (Lantbrukarnas Riksförbund) is a politically independent organization representing farmers, forest owners, and rural entrepreneurs in Sweden.

- 114,000 members  
- Around 9,000 elected representatives (“förtroendevalda”)  
- A large, decentralized structure with local groups operating independently  

LRF works to improve conditions for businesses connected to agriculture, forestry, and rural environments, supporting growth, profitability, and sustainability.

---

## The problem

LRF operates through a wide network of local groups and elected representatives, each with their own activities, budgets, and responsibilities.

This created several challenges:

- Budget management was distributed and difficult to track centrally  
- Manual processes for expenses and compensation  
- Limited visibility into how funds were used across the organization  
- Administrative overhead for handling payments and reporting  
- No structured way for local groups to actively grow their budgets through activities  

The organization needed a system that could handle both **financial control and operational flexibility** across many independent units.

---

## The solution

Demon Dog developed **LRF Kassan**, a custom platform designed specifically for LRF’s structure and workflows.

The system includes:

- Budget management for local groups  
- Salary and compensation handling for elected representatives  
- Expense management and tracking  
- Tools for managing and reporting financial activity  
- Functionality for sales-related activities, allowing local groups to generate income and increase their budgets  

The platform was built to reflect how LRF actually operates—balancing central oversight with local autonomy.

---

## Key features

- Centralized budget tracking across multiple groups  
- Structured handling of compensation and expenses  
- Integration with LRF’s existing member management systems  
- Direct integration with ERP/accounting systems  
- Role-based access for different users and responsibilities  
- Financial reporting and visibility  
- Support for income-generating activities at the local level  
- Scalable structure to support a large number of users and groups  

---

## Outcomes

- Improved control and visibility over budgets and financial activity  
- Reduced administrative workload through structured processes  
- More consistent handling of expenses and compensation  
- Eliminated data duplication through system integrations  
- Enabled local groups to take a more active role in managing and growing their budgets  
- A scalable system aligned with LRF’s organizational structure  

---

## Notes

- This project is a strong example of **Custom Software Development combined with System Integration**  
- The value comes from aligning the system with a **complex, decentralized organization**  
- Important to emphasize both **financial control** and **empowerment of local groups** when describing this case
